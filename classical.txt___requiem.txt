Requiem music is a form of sacred composition often associated with the liturgical tradition of the Christian mass for the deceased. It typically combines somber and reflective tones with powerful orchestration and choral arrangements, evoking themes of mourning, remembrance, and the hope for eternal rest. This genre blends elements of spirituality and emotional depth, often featuring movements like the Introit, Kyrie, Dies Irae, and Agnus Dei, each contributing to the profound and contemplative atmosphere of a requiem mass.
