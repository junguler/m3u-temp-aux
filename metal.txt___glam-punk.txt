Glam punk is a subgenre of punk rock that emerged in the 1970s and 1980s. It combines the raw energy and DIY ethos of punk with the theatricality and flamboyance of glam rock. The music is characterized by catchy hooks, heavy guitar riffs, and lyrics that often deal with themes of rebellion, sexuality, and social commentary.
