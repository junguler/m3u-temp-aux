Bluegrass music is a genre that originated in the Appalachian region of the United States. It is characterized by its fast-paced, intricate instrumental arrangements featuring banjo, fiddle, mandolin, guitar, and upright bass. The genre often features high-pitched vocal harmonies and lyrics that tell stories of rural life, love, and loss.
