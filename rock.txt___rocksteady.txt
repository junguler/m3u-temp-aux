Rocksteady is a music genre that originated in Jamaica in the mid-1960s. It is characterized by a slower tempo than ska music, with a focus on soulful vocals and harmonies, and a strong emphasis on the bass and drums. The lyrics often deal with themes of love, social justice, and political issues. Rocksteady was a precursor to reggae music and had a significant influence on the development of Jamaican music.
