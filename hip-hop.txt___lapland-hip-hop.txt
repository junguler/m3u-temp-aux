Lapland hip hop is a unique subgenre of hip hop that originated in the northernmost region of Finland. It is characterized by its use of traditional Sami music elements, such as the yoik, and incorporates themes of nature, reindeer herding, and the harsh living conditions of the Arctic. The lyrics often touch on social issues and the struggles of living in a remote area. The beats are typically slow and atmospheric, with a focus on storytelling and creating a sense of place.
