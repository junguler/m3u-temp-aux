Early synthpop emerged in the late 1970s and early 1980s as a fusion of electronic and pop music. It was characterized by the use of synthesizers, drum machines, and other electronic instruments to create catchy, danceable melodies and rhythms. The genre was heavily influenced by the experimental electronic music of the 1960s and 1970s, as well as the emerging punk and new wave scenes. Early synthpop bands often incorporated futuristic themes and imagery into their music and visuals, and helped pave the way for the widespread use of electronic instruments in popular music.
