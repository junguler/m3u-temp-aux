House music is a genre of electronic dance music that originated in the early 1980s in Chicago, Illinois. It is characterized by its repetitive 4/4 beat, synthesized melodies, and use of samples and drum machines. House music has evolved over the years, incorporating elements from other genres such as disco, funk, and soul. It is often associated with club culture and is popular in dance clubs and festivals around the world.
